Sean Clarke

Experimental Evolution in the Alm Lab

I am a Biological Engineering graduate student in the Alm lab. Through experimental evolution that began with a wild strain of Vibrio splendidus, I have been instigating genetic diversity and stress tolerance. From genome resequencing the resulting adapted strains, I hope to learn more about the genetic mechanisms of adaptation and how adaption may be engineered in helpful bacteria.
My other interests include food microbiology, industrial design, and effective teaching methods, all of which I would like to combine in future work.
